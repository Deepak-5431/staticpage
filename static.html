The correct answer is: Narmada River
Explanation:

The Narmada River flows between the Vindhyan and Satpura ranges in central India. It is one of the few major rivers in India that flows from east to west, originating from Amarkantak in Madhya Pradesh.


The river flows through a rift valley created between these two mountain ranges, making it geologically significant. It is approximately 1,312 km long and is considered one of the sacred rivers of India.
